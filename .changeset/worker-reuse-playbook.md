**Worker reuse policy** — the orchestration playbook now reads the `reuse` config (shipped earlier) and teaches the LEAD when to carry a planner or implementer across child changes instead of spawning a fresh one every time. Step B.1's persistent-planner rule becomes governed by `reuse.planner`: `auto` keeps today's single-planner-per-run behavior, `never` spawns a fresh planner per propose seeded from `planning-context.md`; the planner's cross-change retire decision now uses the resolved reuse threshold (`resolvePipelineReuseConfig(pipeline).roles.planner`), not the handoff threshold. New Step G.1 defines cross-child implementer reuse: at the prerequisite's review-clean gate the LEAD probes the implementer's transcript and, at or below the resolved reuse threshold, warm-reuses it for the dependent child with a contamination guard (predecessor conventions hold only where the dependent's own artifacts are silent); above it, the worker retires with a `retired-between-children` handoff document (cross-change-transferable knowledge, empty `remaining`) and a fresh implementer is dual-source seeded from that document plus the LEAD's dispatch brief. Reuse requires a unique warm predecessor — DAG merge nodes always get a fresh worker, multi-source seeded from each prerequisite's durable findings — and reused workers carry `reusedFrom: <prerequisite-child-id>` in run-state. The H.3 `DONE` contract gains a durable-findings clause (1–3 lines the LEAD relays verbatim into the next planner's dispatch), and the H.7 session-relay quiesce rule now requires any held warm reuse candidate to write its knowledge digest before the relay. Scope guards: the design-level fixer is excluded (fresh eyes are its value), Tier B / Codex degrade through the existing warm-seed / `threadId`-resume ladders, and reuse across a user's manually-run sequence of unrelated changes is out of scope. Documentation only for CLI surface — no new commands or flags.
